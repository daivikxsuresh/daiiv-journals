Semiconductors — The Commodity Powering the Modern World

April 2025 — The Chip Behind Everything

Semiconductors power smartphones, cars, medical devices, data centers — and supply chain systems themselves. This month deep-dives into semiconductor supply chains: a topic that moved from tech niche to geopolitical flashpoint.

The semiconductor supply chain is extraordinarily complex:

  • Design — Engineers create chip blueprints (Qualcomm, AMD, NVIDIA in the U.S.)
  • Manufacturing — Specialized fabs on silicon wafers (TSMC dominates)
  • Packaging & Testing — Often in Malaysia, Vietnam, South Korea
  • Distribution — Global shipment to device manufacturers

Taiwan produces 60%+ of the world's advanced chips. TSMC alone accounts for most sophisticated semiconductor manufacturing globally. This geographic concentration is a critical supply chain vulnerability.

🏭
The CHIPS Act & Reshoring Reality
$52B to rebuild US semiconductor manufacturing capacity

CHIPS Act & Reshoring

The 2022 CHIPS and Science Act provides $52B to boost U.S. semiconductor manufacturing — a direct response to the COVID-era chip shortage that froze automotive production and disrupted entire industries.

Reshoring in Progress

  • Intel building new fabs in Arizona and Ohio
  • TSMC constructing a major facility in Phoenix
  • Samsung expanding in Texas
  • Micron investing in New York fab expansion

Challenge: U.S. semiconductor fabs cost 30–50% more to build and operate than Asian counterparts. The talent pipeline takes years to build. These are decade-long investments. The CHIPS Act is necessary — but results will take time.

⚠️
Tariffs & The Geopolitical Chess Game
Taxing the very equipment needed to build domestic chip capacity

Tariffs & Geopolitics

Tariffs on semiconductor manufacturing equipment (from the Netherlands' ASML and Japan's Tokyo Electron) increase production costs for U.S.-based chipmakers. Tariffs on Taiwan and South Korea risk straining relationships with critical allies.

The long game: reduce reliance on foreign suppliers, build U.S. chip capacity, foster domestic innovation. Short-term pain is real — but a country controlling its semiconductor supply chain controls its technological and military future.

Stocks to Watch

  • $AMD — Strong CPU and GPU portfolio
  • $NVDA — Dominant in AI accelerators and data center chips
  • $TSMC — The world's most advanced chip manufacturer
  • $AVGO — Broadcom: infrastructure semiconductors and networking
